What actually makes residential proxies fast (or slow)

Raw provider marketing rarely explains real-world speed. For competitor analysis in Belgium, residential proxy performance comes down to a handful of factors you can actually measure and control.

  • Network capacity: well-provisioned pools resist slow-downs under load
  • Routing distance: the closer the exit node to the target, the lower the latency
  • Pool sharing: lightly-shared or dedicated IPs are more predictable than busy shared pools
  • Success rate: blocks force retries, and retries are the hidden tax on speed
  • Concurrency: the right number of parallel connections keeps throughput high without contention

On real workloads, the fastest setup for competitor analysis is the one that scores best on block ratio across many requests — not the one with the flashiest advertised number.
